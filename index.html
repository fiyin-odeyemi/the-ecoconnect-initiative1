Community-led • Data-driven • Youth-powered

Local action, real data, and youth leadership for healthier, climate‑resilient communities.

The EcoConect Initiative equips underserved communities with environmental education, real-time reporting tools, youth Eco‑Ambassadors, and a live dashboard that highlights pollution hotspots, unmanaged waste, and climate risks. The goal is simple: help residents protect their environment, their health, and their future.

Your community, your data

Residents report issues like blocked drainage or unmanaged waste. The app turns that into structured data and guidance.

What is The EcoConect Initiative?

EcoConect is a youth-led platform that blends technology, education, and on‑the‑ground mobilisation. Through the EcoConect app, residents can access learning materials, report environmental risks, and get practical guidance. Youth Eco‑Ambassadors turn that insight into action — engaging households, traders, and local leaders.

  • Community reporting with photos and location tags (blocked drains, waste piles, flooding risks).
  • Easy learning modules on climate, sanitation, flooding, and health.
  • Youth Eco‑Ambassadors who mobilise residents and push for real change.
  • A live dashboard that helps communities and decision‑makers see real trends.

The problem we are solving

In underserved, climate‑vulnerable communities, waste mismanagement, clogged drains, polluted markets, and a lack of environmental education create daily health risks. People are blamed for “poor hygiene,” but they’re rarely given the tools, knowledge, or data to act. Cleanups happen — then everything goes back to the way it was. There is no feedback loop, no ownership, no data.

EcoConect replaces one‑off sensitisation with continuous reporting, education and follow‑up. We help communities see and track their own progress — and hold systems accountable.

How EcoConect works

We unite digital tools with human leadership. The technology gathers and visualises the problems. Trained Eco‑Ambassadors (local youth) organise cleanups, talk with traders and households, promote safer waste practices, and advocate for structural fixes with local authorities. Technology + people = behaviour change that lasts.

Live Community Dashboard
Tracks reports like blocked drainage, uncontrolled dumping, and recurring risk hotspots. Helps leaders target action, not guess.
Eco‑Ambassadors
We train youth (18–30) from within the community — especially women and excluded groups — to teach, mobilise, and monitor. They become trusted local climate leaders.
Education & Behaviour Change
Short, visual lessons inside the app and in-person sessions in markets and compounds. We talk about health, flooding, waste, and everyday prevention — not abstract climate theory.
Evidence for Policy
The dashboard generates reliable, community‑level environmental data for local governments and agencies. Instead of vague complaints, they get clear, geo‑tagged problem maps.

Who benefits

We begin in Ketu, Lagos (6,000+ residents generating 5–6 tons of unmanaged waste daily). Our first‑year goal: reach 1,000 residents and train at least 10 Eco‑Ambassadors in each focus community. We prioritise informal workers, women, young people and those most exposed to environmental risk.

Residents & Market Traders

People living and working near unmanaged waste and blocked drainage get practical guidance and immediate cleanup support — not just blaming.

Youth (18–30)

Young people become Eco‑Ambassadors: educators, organisers, and data stewards. They gain leadership experience and green skills.

Women & Marginalised Groups

We intentionally elevate voices usually excluded from environmental decision-making and frontline planning.

Local Authorities & Policymakers

They receive community‑level evidence on waste, drainage and pollution hotspots — data they can act on immediately.

Our projects

We co‑design action with the people most affected — traders, mothers, young people, and informal workers. Below are examples of our cleanup drives and sensitisation campaigns. These are not photoshoots. They’re working sessions in real markets.

Cleanup Drive & Waste Sensitisation – Ketu Market

We mobilised traders and shoppers to clear blocked drainage, sweep high‑waste areas, and discuss how dumping affects flooding, disease spread, and air quality.

Hands-on Behaviour Change

Instead of lectures, we clean alongside the community. People see results immediately. They keep going after we leave — because it’s theirs, not ours.

Health & Safety Education

We connect waste and flooding to real health outcomes — infections, blocked access, contaminated food. Once people understand the ‘why’, habits shift.

Climate Conversations in Local Language

Our team meets traders where they are — literally and culturally — translating climate and flooding risk into everyday language that actually lands.

One-on-One Market Outreach

We sit with street vendors and small stall owners to talk through waste practices, safer disposal, and why blocked drains hurt everyone — customers included.

Community Ownership

Behaviour change continues after the session. Residents organise themselves to keep shared spaces clean, safer, and less flood-prone.

Community Ownership

Drainage Clearing

Data for Action

This is not just clean-up. It’s evidence. The dashboard shows which streets report unmanaged waste every week, so the right people can respond.
